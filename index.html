What is Musyarakah?

Musyarakah (Joint Venture Profit and Loss Sharing) is a Shariah financing principle based on cooperation between two or more parties to run a specific business or project. Profits and losses are shared based on the percentage of ownership of each party. This partnership model is based on the principles of equity, risk-sharing, and mutual cooperation. In musyarakah, all partners have the right to participate in the management of the business, but can delegate this authority to a managing partner. Musyarakah can be used for various types of projects, including real estate development, trade finance, and manufacturing.





What is Musyarakah's Benefit?

  1. The sharing of profits and losses according to a pre-agreed ratio.
  2. The repayment of the financing principal is adjusted based on the customer's cash flow or business revenue, in order to avoid burdening the customer.
  3. The profit-sharing principle in musyarakah differs from the fixed interest principle, where the bank charges the financing (customer) a fixed amount of interest regardless of the customer's profit, even in the event of losses and economic crises.
